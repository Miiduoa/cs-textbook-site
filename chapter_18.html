Automata

Computing models classify problems by what machines can solve them:

  • Finite Automata: regular languages
  • Pushdown Automata: context‑free languages
  • Turing Machines: general computation

Languages & Grammars

Chomsky hierarchy: Regular ⊂ Context‑Free ⊂ Context‑Sensitive ⊂ Recursively Enumerable.

Decidability

Some problems are undecidable, meaning no algorithm can solve all cases (e.g., the halting problem).
